About the Academy

The academy has expertise, education, and computer software all for the purpose of supporting positive workplace practices.

Unique to the Academy are small consortium groups that are assembled for each new software development project. Advantages for partners may include (1) influence design and functionality (2) marketing rights (3) discounted license fees for internal use and/or (4) goods and services in kind with option to convert value to equity.

Hire from Our National Network

We have an international network of expertise. Many are members of our City Chapters. Their expertise is equal to the best from large well publicized consulting firms. Since many are freelancers or have small companies the fees for our clients do not include large profit margins, overhead, payroll taxes, benefits, or amounts to cover non-billed employee hours.

Our Culture Story ©

The Academy has developed a 6-step process for companies to document and gather their culture practices all in one place. We call the system “Our Culture Story”. The results are (1) a printed booklet with a company’s culture practices (2) an online e-booklet with the same contents and (3) online computer software with the booklet data where employees can add more content such as their experiences, thoughts, beliefs, and opinions for each culture practice. This additional content is available only for employees and is not in the public booklet. We would love to explain this more fully in a short Zoom meeting. Let Jerry know if you would like to do that.

Workplace Team Behaviors

One of our specialites is working with business teams to arrive at the behaviors expected of its members and then measuring their achievement of those behaviors. We use the World Cafe process to guide the team to arrive at and define their behaviors. Then the software for teams collects data to measure achievement of those behaviors. We have designed and documented the product Teams Get It©. This project is ready for a support consortium to be assembled.

Workplace Wisdom Retreat

The Academy sponsors the prestigious Workplace Wisdom retreat. This is a group of culture professionals who are together in support of a common cause. The cause is to share expertise on how to create and sustain positive workplace cultures.

The interactive format with all participants including the speakers is something that isn’t seen anywhere else. It emphasizes friendships with like-minded people and taking home fresh new implementable ideas.

City Chapters

The Academy has chapters in Omaha, NE, Kansas City, MO, Dallas, TX and Denver, CO.  Chapters arrange 2-4 local meetings per year with speakers.  Each Chapter has a President with a leadership team.

The purpose of the chapters is education and friendships that promote positive workplace cultures.

Would you like to start up a venue where you live to meet and share with like-minded people? We would love to help you get started. Send us a note and we’ll be in touch.

Omaha Chapter

The Omaha Chapter continues to drive forward the Academy’s mission of bringing together a community of socially minded workplace culture ambassadors that have an exemplary reputation for sharing practices of excellence for superior workplace joy, productivity and innovation.  The Omaha chapter currently meets quarterly and connects community members from all avenues of business.  Whether you are a business of one or 1,000, have an amazing workplace culture or are striving for one, we welcome you to join us.

Every meeting is hosted by an organization that spotlights their culture adventure.  Through the art of storytelling and the science of tools, each company or individual shares their contribution to the company culture movement.  Questions and pondering are encouraged!

Company Culture Gardening

A few months ago, I thought about company culture being like a family vegetable garden. Both need nurturing, cultivation, fertilizer, sunshine, water and the plants that will produce the desired result. Then I had the good fortune to be introduced to Dr. Irv Rubin. He liked the idea. Now he and I and a few guest authors are writing chapters for a book to be called “A Company Culture Gardeners Guide”. We will publish them one chapter at a time. When finished we’ll put them into a book. If you would like to suggest a chapter that you would like to write, let Jerry know. Or maybe you know someone that would like to. The introductory chapter here introduces “Company Culture Gardens”.

— Jerry Wagner.
