Who We Are

The Academy of Culture Ambassadors is a group of like-minded individuals working to accelerate the growth of kindness and purpose in workplaces. We recognize that the human factor is of the utmost importance in all organizations—without people, they don’t work!.

The Academy is a non-profit corporation. This designation underscores our commitment to providing services for the benefit of the public. Our purpose, “To accelerate the growth of kindness and purpose in workplaces,” is good for everyone.

A uniqueness for the Academy is that we design, develop, and market Culture Support Software that provides for our financial sustainability.

This image captures the essence of what the Academy does and what follows in this web site is an elaboration upon that.

Academy Teams

Academy teams are organized within the Academy for specific purposes. They are within the Academy in contrast to Affiliates described in the next section.

Chapters Team: Chapters are groups in cities. Chapters have been similar in structure to Chapters in professional associations such as SHRM. However, a Chapter may wish to not follow that format. A Chapter can form an assemblage with a few friends that decide what to do and how to do it so long as it supports the Academy’s purpose.

An example of a Chapter is in Omaha, Nebraska. Click their logo to see what they do and how they do it.

   

Teams are being formed within the Academy that are a mix of employees, volunteers, donors, and customers. Each team is a Self-Managed. Simply put, that means the team agrees upon a task(s), each person is accountable, and there is open and honest communication. The purpose of each team is to be a brain trust to the Academy for creative ideas and solutions. As teams are formed, they will be shown here along with purpose and photos of team members. A usual communication format is for a team to have a Zoom meeting every 6-8 weeks with email in between. It is fun!.

The Workplace Wisdom Retreat

The Academy sponsors the Workplace Wisdom retreat. This is a group of workplace culture professionals who join together in support of a common cause. That cause being to share expertise on how to create and sustain humanistic workplace cultures.

The interactive format includes all participants including all speakers as participants. The retreat emphasizes friendships with like-minded people and taking home fresh new implementable ideas.

Our Culture Story© Software

We believe as Howard Schultz the head of Starbucks says: “The currency of leadership is transparency”.

The Academy has designed software that gives all employees ultra-transparency to data that affect their wellbeing in their company culture. That ranges from rating achievement of agreed upon company culture practices to employee conversations about the company’s plans for the future. We call this Our Culture Story©. Nothing is anonymous and all employees are invited to participate. The project is ready for a consortium group as mentioned in the Product Development section. To learn more, click here.

Teams Get It© Software

The Academy has designed software for business teams to arrive at the behaviors expected of its members and then measuring their achievement of those behaviors. We call the software Teams Get It©. To learn more, click here . The project is ready for a support consortium as mentioned in the Product Development section.

Our Ideas© Software

The Academy has designed software for employees to offer ideas for achieving the wellbeing culture they wish for. When ideas reach a threshold of employee likes, they are emailed to a designated management group. Their responses are captured and are available for employee review. This project is ready for a support consortium as mentioned in the Product Development and Marketing Partners section.

Omaha Chapter

The Omaha Chapter continues to drive forward the Academy’s mission of bringing together a community of socially minded workplace culture ambassadors that have an exemplary reputation for sharing practices of excellence for superior workplace joy, productivity and innovation.  The Omaha chapter currently meets quarterly and connects community members from all avenues of business.  Whether you are a business of one or 1,000, have an amazing workplace culture or are striving for one, we welcome you to join us.

Every meeting is hosted by an organization that spotlights their culture adventure.  Through the art of storytelling and the science of tools, each company or individual shares their contribution to the company culture movement.  Questions and pondering are encouraged!

Leadership and Members

The Omaha chapter is led by Stephanie Roob, a Director at QLI, the Nation’s premier post-hospital rehab center for brain injury and spinal cord injury and EIGHT time Best Places to Work winner.  Stephanie is also the Secretary for the National Academy of Culture Ambassadors.

Other Omaha committee members include Laurie Pieper, Director of Human Resources at the Greater Omaha Chamber of Commerce, Pernell Toney, Director or Talent Analytics at Blue Cross and Blue Shield of Nebraska and Christy Johnston, Senior Director of Culture and Engagement at Medical Solutions.

To learn more about the Omaha Chapter and to join the mailing list, email Stephanie at sroob@qliomaha.com or omahacultureambassadors@gmail.com.
