Workplace Team Behaviors

Our specialty is working with business teams to arrive at the behaviors expected of its members and then measuring their achievement of those behaviors. We use the World Cafe process to guide the team to arrive at and define their behaviors. Then the software for teams collects data to measure achievement of those behaviors. We have designed and documented the product Teams Get It. This project is ready for a support consortium to be assembled.

Document Company Cultures

The “field” of business culture is so broad that it is nearly impossible for a company to be aware of the many possible practices. We have designed and documented an interactive system for companies to document their existing culture practices and then share them with all employees. The system also shows what other practices and tools a company might consider. This project is ready for a support consortium to be assembled. Click here for more details.

Workplace Wisdom Retreat

The Academy sponsors the prestigious Workplace Wisdom retreat. This is a group of culture professionals who are together in support of a common cause. The cause is to share expertise on how to create and sustain positive workplace cultures.

The interactive format with all participants including the speakers is something that isn’t seen anywhere else. It emphasizes friendships with like-minded people and taking home fresh new implementable ideas.

Company Culture Gardening

A few months ago, I thought about company culture being like a family vegetable garden. Both need nurturing, cultivation, fertilizer, sunshine, water and the plants that will produce the desired result. Then I had the good fortune to be introduced to Dr. Irv Rubin. He liked the idea. Now he and I and a few guest authors are writing chapters for a book to be called “A Company Culture Gardeners Guide”. We will publish them one chapter at a time. When finished we’ll put them into a book. If you would like to suggest a chapter that you would like to write, let Jerry know. Or maybe you know someone that would like to. The introductory chapter here introduces “Company Culture Gardens”.

— Jerry Wagner.
